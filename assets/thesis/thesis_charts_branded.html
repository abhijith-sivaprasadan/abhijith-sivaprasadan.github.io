Both geometries lose efficiency at higher pressure ratios (Case A → C). The 2 pp legacy advantage at Case A is a surface-area artifact: the compact two-step body exposes less wall to ambient, not better internal heat transfer — confirmed by the insulated reference cases.

Redesigned: Case A 53.4W, Case B 56.6W, Case C 57.2W. Legacy: Case A 18.6W, Case B 18.9W, Case C 18.9W.

The redesigned reducer's 150 mm quintic contraction exposes significantly more wall area to ambient than the compact two-step body. This is the primary trade-off: the quintic geometry recovers total pressure and eliminates the supersonic vena contracta, but at the cost of higher external thermal loss. The trade-off is the engineering decision this thesis quantifies.
