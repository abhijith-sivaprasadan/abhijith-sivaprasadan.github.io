y+ validation: Legacy 0.5-11.2 with step spike; New 0.34→10.6 monotonic. Both acceptable for k-omega SST.

Both geometries achieve y⁺ values within the acceptable range for k-ω SST with enhanced wall treatment (target y⁺ < 5 for full viscous sublayer resolution, up to ~11 acceptable in the buffer layer with automatic wall function blending). The legacy geometry shows a spike at the step zone where the vena contracta produces intense near-wall shear. The new quintic reducer shows a monotonically increasing y⁺ as the flow accelerates through the contraction, rising from 0.34 at the inlet to ~10 at the throat — consistent with the velocity increase predicted by the Mach number profile. Both datasets confirm that the prismatic inflation layers maintain adequate near-wall resolution throughout.

Static T: Legacy 673→583K sharp drop at step (vena contracta) then 606K recovery. New 673→625K smooth descent.

The static temperature profiles are the isentropic complement to the Mach number profiles. As the gas accelerates, kinetic energy increases at the expense of thermal energy, so static temperature falls. The legacy geometry produces a 90 K static temperature drop concentrated at the vena contracta (from ~669 K to ~583 K), matching the isentropic prediction for Ma ≈ 0.91: T_static = T_total × (1 + 0.2Ma²)⁻¹ ≈ 673 × 0.861 ≈ 579 K. Following the vena contracta, the gas decelerates in the outlet pipe and static temperature partially recovers to ~606 K. The redesigned geometry produces a smooth 48 K decrease from 673 K to 625 K along the 150 mm quintic contraction, with no recovery spike, consistent with monotonic near-choked acceleration through the geometric throat. The local temperature minimum at the vena contracta is significant for measurement accuracy: a pressure sensor at that location sees a gas temperature 90 K below the supply temperature, affecting density assumptions in dynamic pressure calibration.
