Both geometries show well-converged solutions over 1,500 iterations. The residual difference between the two cases is physically meaningful rather than a convergence quality issue. The legacy reducer's higher final k and ω residuals reflect the turbulent jet and vena contracta structure at the two-step contraction: the intense shear and localised supersonic pocket sustain higher turbulence production, which the k-ω SST model must resolve at every iteration. The new quintic contraction has no separation or jet, producing lower and more uniform turbulence levels throughout, and correspondingly lower turbulence equation residuals. Convergence in both cases was additionally confirmed through physics-based KPI monitors (net pressure drop, outlet temperature, mass flow balance) which all stabilised to within ±0.06 Pa over the final 100 iterations.
