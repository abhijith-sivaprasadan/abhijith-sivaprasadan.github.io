The legacy two-step geometry produces a vena contracta at both abrupt contraction steps, with the combined effect creating a locally supersonic pocket at Ma 0.908 approximately 62 mm from the inlet. The smooth C²-continuous quintic contraction eliminates both separation zones: the Mach number rises monotonically with no spike, confirming that the effective throat coincides with the geometric throat. Note: the two datasets use different outlet pressure boundary conditions, explaining the difference in downstream Mach level.

Legacy: 100000 Pa → 96312 Pa (loss 3688 Pa). Redesigned: 100000 Pa → 99832 Pa (loss 168 Pa). 22× improvement.

The legacy reducer loses 3,688 Pa through cumulative Borda–Carnot losses at two abrupt contractions, leaving the downstream sensor operating at 96.3 kPa effective supply pressure rather than the nominal 100 kPa — a systematic calibration error of 3.7%. The redesigned quintic contraction loses only 168 Pa, preserving 99.8 kPa at the sensor inlet. This is the core engineering result of the thesis: the smooth C²-continuous contraction eliminates irreversible pressure loss, enabling accurate dynamic pressure sensor calibration.
