Legacy reducer Mach number: 0.016 at inlet, peak 0.908 at vena contracta (x≈227mm), settling at 0.79 at outlet.

The legacy two-step geometry creates cumulative Borda–Carnot losses at each abrupt contraction. The centerline Mach number peaks at 0.908 — exceeding sonic conditions locally at the vena contracta despite subcritical nominal pressure ratios — confirming that the effective throat is displaced downstream of the geometric throat. The flow then recovers to approximately 0.79 in the Ø6 mm outlet pipe.

Legacy reducer total pressure: 100000 Pa at inlet, drops at step geometry, settles at ~96312 Pa at outlet — loss of 3688 Pa.

Total pressure remains at 100,000 Pa through the large-diameter inlet pipe, confirming negligible friction losses upstream of the contractions. The sharp drop at the step geometry zone (x ≈ 220–228 mm) marks the two cumulative Borda–Carnot loss events. The outlet total pressure of 96,312 Pa means the sensor sees a reduced effective supply pressure, directly affecting calibration accuracy of the Pulsatorn rig — the core problem this thesis addresses.
