Wall temperature: Legacy 671.6K plateau then sharp drop to 639K at 26mm; New smooth 673K→648K over 150mm.

The wall temperature profiles directly reflect the flow physics identified in the Mach number analysis. The legacy two-step geometry produces a near-isothermal wall upstream of the steps (~671 K, within 2 K of the gas inlet temperature), then a sharp 32 K drop concentrated in approximately 12 mm around the step zone where the vena contracta and turbulent jet drive intense localised heat transfer. The redesigned quintic contraction distributes the same energy exchange over 150 mm: wall temperature decreases monotonically from 673 K at the contraction inlet to 648 K at the throat, at an average rate of ~0.17 K/mm versus ~2.7 K/mm at the legacy step — a factor of 16 difference in local thermal gradient. This concentrated wall cooling in the legacy geometry is consistent with the Biot number analysis (Bi = 0.003–0.004): the solid wall temperature closely follows the local gas temperature, so the abrupt gas cooling at the vena contracta is reflected almost immediately in the wall profile. Note: the legacy dataset covers only the step region (0–38 mm); the redesigned dataset covers the full 150 mm quintic contraction zone.
