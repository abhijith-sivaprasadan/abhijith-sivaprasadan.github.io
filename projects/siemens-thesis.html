Siemens Energy CFD/CHT Thesis - Gas Turbine Reducer Thermo-Fluid Analysis

Seven months embedded at Siemens Energy Finspång conducting compressible CFD and conjugate heat transfer analysis of reducer geometries for the Pulsatorn high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig.

Project snapshot

  • Organisation: Siemens Energy AB, Fluid Dynamic Lab, Finspång
  • Tools: ANSYS Fluent, k-omega SST, NI-DAQ, LabVIEW, Siemens NX, Teamcenter PLM2020, ANSYS SpaceClaim
  • Focus: CFD/CHT, compressible flow, pressure-loss analysis, thermal delivery, thermocouple/pressure measurement, validation planning, design iteration

What I did

  • Commissioned and verified NI-DAQ measurement-chain elements for preliminary rig tests, including thermocouple and static/dynamic pressure channels.
  • Developed steady-state compressible CFD/CHT models in ANSYS Fluent using k-omega SST turbulence modelling at inlet T = 673 K and P_gauge = 100,000 Pa.
  • Ran a three-level mesh independence study and confirmed discretisation independence to within 2% before the full simulation campaign.
  • Applied Biot number analysis (Bi = 0.003-0.004) and compared baseline and redesign cases to separate thermal behaviour from flow-regime effects.
  • Documented assumptions and limitations, then defined next-step validation needs for transient/pulsating operation and experimental verification.

Relevance

Why this matters

This work strengthens my modelling profile with practical engineering validation experience: connecting simulations, measurements, uncertainty, test rigs and documentation. The experimental phase also included independent thermal testing up to 700°C and formal root cause analysis after heater failure.
