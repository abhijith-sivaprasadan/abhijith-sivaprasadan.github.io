CFD / CHT

Siemens Energy CFD/CHT Thesis – Gas Turbine Reducer Thermo-Fluid Analysis

Compressible CFD and conjugate heat transfer analysis of reducer geometries for the Pulsatorn high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig at Siemens Energy Finspång.

ANSYS Fluent / NI-DAQ / LabVIEW / Siemens NX Featured
ANSYS FluentCFD/CHTGas TurbinesNI-DAQLabVIEWSiemens NXk-omega SST
CFD/CHT modelSteady compressible coupled workflow NI-DAQ/LabVIEW validationMeasurement-chain commissioning Pressure-loss + thermal splitBiot-based interpretation
  • Built steady-state compressible CFD and CHT models in ANSYS Fluent (k-omega SST) at T = 673 K and P_gauge = 100 kPa within one Siemens Energy thesis campaign.
  • Mesh independence study confirmed discretisation error below 2% on pressure-loss coefficient and peak Mach number.
  • Biot number analysis (Bi = 0.003-0.004) separated thermal contributions from flow-regime differences between reducer designs.
  • Commissioned NI-DAQ measurement chain, modified LabVIEW VI, and led root cause analysis of heater failure during 700°C testing.
