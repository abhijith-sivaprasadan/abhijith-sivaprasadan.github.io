Methodology & equations

Methodology & equations
  • Area-Mach + isentropic: Anderson Ch.3-5 — staticToTotalPressure for p_e/p₀
  • Bartz local h_g: h_g/h_g* ≈ (A_t/A)^0.9 — NASA TN-19650013685 (worker-side fluid-sim-worker.js:280)
  • Resistance network: R_total = 1/h_g + t_cu/k_cu + δ_dep/k_dep + 1/h_cool (worker-side fluid-sim-worker.js:256)
  • Conductance health: h/h_clean = R_clean/R_total — conductanceHealthRatio
  • Deposit share: R_dep/R_total — depositResistanceShare
  • Coolant ΔT: q″·A_wall/(ṁ·cp_CH₄) — coolantTemperatureRise. Reference closure: A_wall=0.0025 m², ṁ=1.2 kg/s, cp=2400 J/kgK
  • Burnout limit: T_burnout = 1170 K (OFHC copper, AMS 4500) — burnoutMargin
  • Stanton: St = h_g/(ρ·V·cp) — stantonNumber
  • Time-to-margin: τ = ΔT_burn / (dT_w/dt) from 12-sample rolling buffer

Methodology & equations
  • EnPI: ISO 50006:2014 §5; EnPI = E_purchased / production — energyPerformanceIndicator
  • SEC: ISO 50001:2018 Annex A — specificEnergyConsumption
  • Carnot HP COP: COP = η_2nd·T_sink/(T_sink − T_source); η_2nd = 0.50 (Arpagaus et al., RSER 2018); T_source = 10 °C ambient — heatPumpCarnotCOP
  • Capacity-limited merit order: HP up to 2.0 MW, EB trim up to 1.8 MW, gas balance — dispatchMeritOrder
  • Heat-recovery curve: η_HR = 0.18 + 0.04·load (effectiveness floor + load slope) — heatRecoveryEffectiveness
  • Emissions: weighted (E_el·g_grid + E_gas·g_gas)/production — emissionsIntensity
  • Scopes: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard — scopeSplit
  • MAC: ΔOPEX_annual / ΔCO₂_avoided; el = 850 SEK/MWh, gas = 350 SEK/MWh_LHV, 7200 h/y — marginalAbatementCost

  • Boundaries: Q_dem = 5.2 MW × load; production = 10 u/h × load; parasitic = 320 kW × load

Research interests

Thermal-fluid problems where experiment, simulation and measurement converge.

The Siemens Energy thesis established a working method: build a defensible numerical model, validate the assumptions that can be tested, and state the implications for engineering decisions. The natural research extensions from that foundation are below.

Conjugate heat transfer in high-temperature cooling passages

Steady-state CHT in the thesis demonstrated how geometry, insulation and external heat loss interact in a confined high-temperature flow problem. The next research layer is transient operation, pulsating flow and near-choked flow stability — all relevant to calibration rigs and propulsion hardware where boundary conditions are dynamic.

ANSYS Fluent CHTk-omega SSTBi decompositionTransient CHT

Deposit and surface-condition effects on heat transfer

The thesis treated wall surfaces as clean and fixed. Real high-temperature passages develop altered surface conditions over time, which changes thermal resistance and heat transfer predictions. Connecting deposit-formation mechanisms to measurable thermal indicators is a research direction the thesis methodology is well positioned to support.
