Siemens Energy CFD/CHT Thesis - Gas Turbine Reducer Thermo-Fluid Analysis

Seven months embedded at Siemens Energy Finspång conducting compressible CFD and conjugate heat transfer analysis of reducer geometries for the Pulsatorn high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig.

  • Built steady-state compressible CFD and CHT models in ANSYS Fluent (k-omega SST) for inlet T = 673 K and P_gauge = 100,000 Pa within one Siemens Energy thesis campaign.
  • Conducted a three-level mesh independence study confirming discretisation independence to within 2% before the full simulation campaign.
  • Applied Biot number analysis (Bi = 0.003-0.004) to separate thermal contributions and identified near-choked versus supersonic vena contracta behaviour across geometries.

Test Engineer / Master's Thesis Student - Siemens Energy AB

Finspång, Sweden · Apr 2025 - Nov 2025

Seven months embedded at Siemens Energy Finspång in the Fluid Dynamic Lab. Conducted numerical investigation of steady-state thermo-fluid performance of a reducer geometry for a high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig (Pulsatorn). Built compressible CFD and conjugate heat transfer models in ANSYS Fluent (k-omega SST), conducted three-level mesh independence study, and applied Biot number analysis to decompose thermal performance. Identified flow-regime asymmetry between geometry variants (Ma 0.990 near-choked vs Ma 1.006 supersonic vena contracta). Also commissioned NI-DAQ measurement chains, modified LabVIEW VIs, ran independent test campaigns at up to 700°C, and conducted a formal root cause analysis of a heater failure resulting in an approved project scope change. Used Siemens NX and Teamcenter PLM2020 throughout. Supervisor: Prof. Jens Fridh, KTH. Thesis: TRITA-ITM-EX 2026:14.
