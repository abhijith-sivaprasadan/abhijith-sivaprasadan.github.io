Siemens Energy · Test engineering · CFD/CHT

Numerical Investigation of Steady-State Thermo-Fluid Performance of a Reducer for a High-Temperature Dynamic Pressure Sensor Calibration Rig

TRITA-ITM-EX 2026:14. Seven months embedded in the Fluid Dynamic Lab at Siemens Energy Finspång working on the Pulsatorn gas turbine test rig, ANSYS Fluent CFD/CHT modelling, NI-DAQ instrumentation and high-temperature validation.

Role snapshot

  • Organisation: Siemens Energy AB, Fluid Dynamic Lab, Finspång
  • Period: Apr 2025 - Nov 2025
  • Tools: ANSYS Fluent, ANSYS SpaceClaim, Siemens NX, Teamcenter PLM2020, NI-DAQ, LabVIEW
  • Methods: compressible CFD, conjugate heat transfer, k-omega SST, mesh independence, Biot number analysis
  • Measurements: thermocouples, static pressure and dynamic pressure channels, high-temperature test campaigns

What I contributed

  • Worked on the numerical investigation of steady-state thermo-fluid performance for a reducer geometry used in a high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig.
  • Managed the CFD workflow independently from geometry reconstruction in ANSYS SpaceClaim through structured meshing with prismatic inflation layers, solver setup, convergence monitoring and post-processing.
  • Built steady-state compressible CFD and CHT models in ANSYS Fluent using the k-omega SST turbulence model. Inlet conditions were T = 673 K and P_gauge = 100,000 Pa, with three outlet pressure cases per geometry across six adiabatic and six CHT simulations.
  • Conducted a three-level mesh independence study and confirmed discretisation independence to within 2% before moving to the full simulation campaign.
  • Applied Biot number analysis (Bi = 0.003-0.004) to separate thermal behaviour from flow effects and identified asymmetry between geometries, including Ma 0.990 near-choked behaviour versus Ma 1.006 supersonic vena-contracta behaviour.
  • Commissioned NI-DAQ measurement chains, modified LabVIEW VIs for data acquisition, ran independent thermal test campaigns at up to 700°C and carried out formal root cause analysis of a heater failure that triggered an approved scope change.

Transferable value

Why this experience matters

This role connects modelling with real test constraints: instrumentation, uncertainty, validation planning, documentation and communication between simulation, design iteration and hardware workflows. It is the strongest evidence on the site for gas turbine heat transfer, CFD/CHT and high-temperature test engineering roles.
