Alleima · Energy efficiency · Comparative audit methods

Energy Efficiency Intern - Alleima AB

Industrial energy-performance mapping, KPI/EnPI development, metering-readiness assessment and comparative audit-method framing across global sites.

Role snapshot

  • Organisation: Alleima AB
  • Period: Dec 2024 - Jun 2025
  • Mode: Remote / Sandviken, Sweden
  • Focus: EnPI logic, load drivers, normalisation, metering readiness, loss analysis, comparative audit methods

What I contributed

  • Built an ISO 50001/EED-aligned mapping approach for industrial energy follow-up.
  • Defined KPI/EnPI structures, relevant load drivers, baseline logic and normalisation concepts.
  • Assessed metering and data readiness across sites, including signals, sampling needs, baselines and follow-up reliability.
  • Developed workflows to compare operating states, identify loss mechanisms and frame roadmap opportunities across utilities, process heat, compressed air and heat recovery.
  • Contributed to the comparative regulatory report covering Sweden, Germany, the Netherlands, Czechia, India, China and the United States.

Method

How the EnPI mapping work was structured

  1. Site walk-down + meter inventory: document every utility meter (electricity, gas, steam, compressed air, cooling water), its scan interval and its current point of integration to site SCADA or to manual logs.
  2. Process boundary definition: classify each meter by GHG Protocol scope and by ISO 50006 energy use type. Identify metering gaps where a significant energy use (SEU) is implied but no direct meter exists.
  3. Load-driver candidates: for each EnPI candidate, propose load drivers (production volume, operating hours, ambient temperature, raw-material mix) and check which combination minimises baseline residual variance.
  4. Baseline regression: fit E = a + b·P + c·HDD or similar on the cleanest 12–18 months of data. Validate residuals are stationary; flag periods where they aren't (planned maintenance, batch-mix changes).
  5. Metering-readiness scoring: rate each EnPI from 0–4 on a custom scale: data resolution, time-stamp reliability, missing-data fraction and operator-visible baseline. Anything below 3 is flagged as needing meter upgrade before EnPI can be reported externally.
  6. Comparative framing: overlay Swedish EKL/EED reporting practice against German, Dutch, Czech, Indian, Chinese and US equivalents to identify which audit methods Alleima could re-use across its global plants.

Deliverables

What I left behind

  • EnPI catalogue covering utilities + process heat with formulas, drivers, baseline equation and readiness score per indicator.
  • Metering-gap register listing measurement points to install before each indicator can be reported externally with confidence.
  • Comparative regulatory matrix (7 countries × 6 method dimensions) supporting cross-plant audit-method selection.
  • Loss-analysis worksheets isolating compressed-air leaks, idle-mode utility draw and heat-recovery candidates.
  • One-page summary briefs designed for plant-manager review, with the technical detail moved to appendix and the decision moved to the headline.

Reflection

What I learned about industrial energy work

  • Metering is the gate. No EnPI is more credible than the meter it rests on. Half of the work was sequencing meter upgrades before chasing energy improvements.
  • Baseline normalisation is the lever. Without driver normalisation, a savings claim from a heat-recovery project gets buried by a busy production week. ISO 50006-style EnPI fixes this.
  • Cross-plant comparisons are political. Plant-to-plant benchmarks require enough methodology consistency that the regional energy lead trusts them — and that means an audit-method specification, not just a number.
  • The decision comes before the data. The most useful question I learned to ask: "What decision will this indicator support?" If the answer is unclear, the indicator is not worth the effort.

Transferable value

Why this experience matters

This experience shows how I turn industrial energy context into practical measurement, KPI and decision-support structures for technical and management audiences.

It also fed the longer comparative report on global audit methods, where Sweden’s EKL and EED model was translated into a tiered framework for sites with very different legal and technical maturity. The methodology — boundary, baseline, drivers, residual diagnostics, metering readiness — is the same one that anchors the EnPI toolkit and the EU ETS calculator elsewhere in the portfolio.
