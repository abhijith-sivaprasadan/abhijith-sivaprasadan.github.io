About

Thermal-fluid and energy systems engineer based in Solna, Sweden.

Abhijith is an M.Sc. Sustainable Energy Engineering candidate at KTH Royal Institute of Technology with embedded Siemens Energy thesis work in gas turbine CFD, conjugate heat transfer and test instrumentation. The same profile also covers ISO 50001-style energy management, Python-based modelling and optimisation, and industrial problem solving.

Current snapshot

Current technical direction

My current focus has two connected tracks. The research track is thermal-fluid mechanics of high-temperature flow systems: how deposit formation and surface condition change heat transfer behaviour, how CHT models can be connected to measurable rig quantities, and how monitoring indicators can be derived from simulation and measurement data. The industrial R&D track is decarbonization and electrification: energy/process modelling, optimization, techno-economic reasoning, prototype tools and decision-support methods for industrial utilities such as electrical systems, compressed air, heating and cooling.

  • Current location: Solna, Sweden
  • Primary differentiator: Siemens Energy Finspång thesis on a high-temperature dynamic pressure sensor calibration rig
  • Secondary track: energy systems modelling, optimisation and energy management

Working style

Validate assumptions early

Prefer clear boundary conditions, documented limitations and sanity checks before scaling a model or a test setup.

Connect analysis to hardware

Keep instrumentation, test constraints and model interpretation tied together instead of treating simulation as a standalone exercise.

Move between disciplines

Switch between thermal-fluid engineering, energy systems and software implementation without changing the standard of evidence.
